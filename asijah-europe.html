Category Opportunity Brief, South Indian and Kerala Ambient Food

Current Range Analysis

Asijah-Europe has built a focused and coherent South Indian pantry offering, anchored by the Tamil Nadu category leaders: Aachi for masalas and spice blends, Sakthi for spice powders, and Idhayam for sesame oil. The range speaks clearly to a Tamil diaspora buyer base and covers the cooking essentials those households reach for every week, from sambar powder and biryani masala to rice varieties and lentil staples.

The breadth across staples is real, and the distribution footprint across NL, BE, DE, NO, SE, and DK shows a genuinely European-scale operation. What the range does not yet contain is the ambient snack and pickle category that sits alongside those staples in every South Indian household.

The Commercial Gap
Aachi, Sakthi, and Idhayam cover the Tamil Nadu pantry. The Kerala ambient snack shelf — banana chips, tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, regional mixtures — is absent from your entire range, and so is any Kerala-origin pickle brand to sit alongside it.
The Minzelis Solution

Minzelis fills the Kerala ambient category that Aachi and Sakthi, by design, do not cover. Both are Tamil Nadu masala brands built for the cooking pantry. Neither makes banana chips, tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, or the Kerala-style pickles that complete a South Indian snack shelf. That is not a gap in their quality — it is simply not their category.

Minzelis is Kerala-origin, produced in Calicut, with 105 SKUs across snacks, pickles, pulses, breakfast staples, chutney powders, and pantry items. The snack range alone runs to 51 SKUs with multiple banana chip variants, tapioca formats, regional mixtures, and murukku lines that have no current equivalent in the Asijah-Europe portfolio.

Minzelis is managed from Germany and backed by Gramarhein's export-proven infrastructure, actively shipping to 16 countries, giving you an EU-compliant route to capture this category gap.
