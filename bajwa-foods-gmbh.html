Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

Bajwa Foods has built a strong South Asian packaged food operation across Germany, carrying the brands your customers trust — Gits, Shan, TRS, Vatika, and Heera, alongside your own-label commodity staples. That breadth across North Indian and Pakistani categories gives you genuine reach into the South Asian diaspora in Hessen and beyond.

The Gap We Noticed

There is a segment your current range does not reach: Kerala and South Indian ambient snacks and pickles. None of the brands you stock produce this category. Gits covers ready meals. Shan is spice mixes and meal kits. TRS handles pulses, flours, and pantry staples. Vatika is hair care. Not one of them makes banana chips, jackfruit chips, murukku, or a Kerala-style mango pickle.

For the South Indian and Keralite households in your customer base, that means a full shopping category goes unfilled every time they visit a store supplied by Bajwa. They either import informally, find it online, or travel further to specialist retailers. That is a category gap with a ready customer base already in the market.

It is structural, not accidental. The North Indian food export ecosystem simply has not built it out. The brands are not there yet — which is exactly the opportunity.

Why This Category Moves

The Keralite and South Indian diaspora in Germany is not a niche. It is one of the fastest-growing segments within the broader South Asian community, concentrated heavily in urban Hessen, Baden-Württemberg, and NRW. These are households with purchasing habits built around specific regional foods — foods that taste like home, not a pan-Indian approximation of it.

The crossover effect matters too. Banana chips and murukku are not known exclusively to South Indian buyers. They have a broader audience among general Asian food enthusiasts, existing customers who already trust Bajwa for their Shan masalas and TRS dals and will naturally pick up a Kerala snack from the same shelf.

The category sells in ambient, at normal shelf margins, with no refrigeration or short shelf-life pressures. It sits cleanly alongside the range you already run.
