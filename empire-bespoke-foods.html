Category Opportunity Brief, South Indian and Kerala Ambient Food

Current Range Analysis

Your Indian category is built around meal solutions and drinks. Master Cook brings plant-based dals and meal kits to the South Asian shopper, Mother's Recipe covers condiments and spice pastes, and Hajoori handles the drinks shelf. These brands together serve the Indian shopper at the moment of meal preparation.

What the range does not yet do is catch that same shopper on every other trip, the everyday basket where ambient snacks and pickles travel independently of any recipe occasion.

The Commercial Gap
The South Indian ambient snack and pickle category is entirely absent from your Indian range. Your accounts are sourcing jackfruit chips, banana chips, Kerala mixtures, and table pickles from elsewhere, every week, without your margin in the transaction.
The Minzelis Solution

Minzelis fills this category gap as a complete range rather than a single SKU. Fifty-one ambient snack SKUs and ten Kerala-style pickles, all origin-specific, all ambient, all designed for the retail and wholesale shelf. These are daily-repeat items for South Asian shoppers, banana chips, tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, pakkavada, Kerala mixtures, and table pickles that move independently of meal occasions and land in the basket every week.

The pickles in your current Indian range sit in the condiment and spice-paste space. Minzelis pickles are a separate format entirely: table pickles eaten as an accompaniment, in the same shelf location where your accounts currently buy from other suppliers. They complement Mother's Recipe rather than competing with it.

Minzelis is managed from Germany and backed by Gramarhein's export-proven infrastructure, actively shipping to 16 countries, giving you an EU-compliant route to capture this category gap.
