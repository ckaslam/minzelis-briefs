Category Opportunity Brief, South Indian and Kerala Ambient Food

Current Range Analysis

Herman Kuijper's portfolio reflects a clear and deliberate strategy: to be the reference importer for East and Southeast Asian food across Europe, with exceptional depth across Japanese, Vietnamese, Korean, Thai, and Chinese categories. The Indian segment sits as a recent extension of that core offer, currently anchored by TRS commodity staples — gramflour and coconut oil — functional ingredients that speak to a general Asian pantry rather than to the specific South Indian consumer looking for branded, origin-driven food.

That distinction matters. Commodity staples are entry points. The South Indian ambient food market — snacks, pickles, breakfast staples, pantry items — is a branded, loyalty-driven category where the product itself carries the heritage signal. TRS coconut oil does not compete in the same purchase occasion as Kerala banana chips or a Kaduku Mango pickle.

The Commercial Gap
A portfolio covering 10,000 Asian SKUs with no South Indian snack or pickle line leaves the fastest-growing Indian diaspora segment in Europe without a reason to stay loyal to your range.
The Minzelis Solution

The South Indian ambient snack category has no natural home in the current major Asian food distribution networks. TRS, the dominant Indian food brand in European wholesale, does not produce Kerala-origin snacks or South Indian pickles. That gap is structural, not accidental — and it means the customers seeking banana chips, jackfruit chips, or a lime pickle made in Kerala are currently sourcing from fragmented, smaller brands or simply not finding them in mainstream wholesale channels at all.
