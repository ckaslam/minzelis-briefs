Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

Jamoona has built one of Europe's strongest D2C Indian grocery platforms, with a well-stocked range spanning Haldiram's, Daawat, Aashirvaad, Priya, Mother's Recipe, and Ashoka across snacks, staples, and pickles. The breadth you offer across North Indian and pan-Indian categories is clear, and it shows in the 150,000+ customers you serve across Germany and the Netherlands.

The Gap We Noticed

Your snack range covers banana chips, but the wider Kerala ambient category is missing. Tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, Kerala mixtures, pakkavada, murukku — these are products that Kerala and broader South Indian diaspora customers in NL and DE actively search for, and currently cannot find on jamoona.com as a named Kerala brand.

The pickle side tells the same story. Your current pickle range runs Priya (Andhra), Mother's Recipe (North Indian), Ashoka, and Ahmed — strong brands for North and pan-Indian buyers. There is no Kerala-style pickle in the mix. Tender mango, lime, gooseberry, ginger — these are distinct from North Indian pickles and serve a different palate entirely.

For a platform with your audience size in Germany and the Netherlands, a Kerala-origin ambient brand would close the one South Indian segment that currently has no dedicated presence.

Why This Category Moves

The South Indian diaspora in Germany and the Netherlands is a distinct and growing buyer segment. For Kerala and Tamil families in particular, the snacks they grew up with are not interchangeable with North Indian namkeens. Jackfruit chips, tapioca sticks, and Kerala mixture occupy a very specific taste memory — customers who know these products buy them repeatedly, and they tend to seek them out rather than settle.

The category is already proven in UK and German Indian retail. What is less established is a branded Kerala ambient range that arrives shelf-ready for a European platform, with consistent supply and proper EU labelling. That is the commercial opening.

Your existing customers browsing jamoona.com and finding Haldiram's Dakshin Banana Chips are the same customers who would add a Kerala tapioca chips or a tender mango pickle to their basket if it were there. The demand is already in your traffic — it just has no product to land on yet.
