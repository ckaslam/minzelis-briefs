Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

Kreyenhop & Kluge carries one of the most complete Asian food portfolios in Germany — 3,500+ SKUs across Thailand, China, Japan, Vietnam, Korea, and India, from brands including Rajah, Patak's, Tilda, India Gate, Ashoka, and Truly Indian. Your India section covers the core categories well: spices and masalas, curry pastes, basmati rice, and North Indian convenience food. It is a serious range, assembled over decades.

The Gap We Noticed

Across your entire 3,500-SKU assortment, there is no South Indian or Kerala-origin product. Not one ambient snack, not one regional pickle, not one item from the southern quarter of the subcontinent.

Rajah covers ground spices and North Indian pickles. Patak's covers curry pastes and cooking sauces. Tilda and India Gate cover basmati rice. Ashoka covers North and pan-Indian ready meals. Truly Indian explicitly positions as a North Indian producer. The India section is well-stocked for one cuisine — and structurally silent on another.

Kerala ambient snacks — banana chips, tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, regional mixtures — are a distinct product category that none of the above brands produce. They sell to a distinct buyer, sit on a distinct shelf, and move independently of the curry-sauce category entirely. The gap exists not because K&K has overlooked a product, but because no supplier has brought the right range to the conversation yet.

Why This Category Moves

Germany has approximately 210,000 people of South Asian origin, with a significant Kerala and Tamil population concentrated in NRW, Bavaria, and the Rhine-Main region. These consumers shop in Asian specialist stores — the same channel K&K supplies. For this buyer group, banana chips, murukku, and jackfruit chips are not a novelty snack. They are everyday staples that taste like home, and they actively look for them.

The commercial dynamic is additive, not substitutive. A South Indian shopper looking for banana chips is not browsing the Patak's shelf. They are looking at a separate fixture, or leaving the store without buying. Adding a South Indian ambient range does not cannibalise anything that is already moving — it fills an aisle gap that currently sends buyers elsewhere.

The distribution side has been the bottleneck, not the demand side. Rajah and Patak's have dominated the India section in German ethnic trade for years because they had the logistics and the EU compliance to supply at scale. The same infrastructure is now available for Kerala ambient food through Minzelis, backed by an export operation already shipping to 16 countries.
