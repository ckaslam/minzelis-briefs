Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

KRG Asian Food carries one of the strongest Indian grocery portfolios in the Netherlands. Brands like MTR, Aashirvaad, Priya, Heera, Lijjat, TRS, and Everest give your range genuine depth across spices, flours, ready meals, and pickles. It's a range that clearly serves an Indian diaspora customer base, not a tokenistic one.

Your snacks section covers popular North Indian formats well — Balaji Wafers, Kurkure, Lays, and Karachi Bakery. Your pickle category is active. The range is built by someone who understands the market.

The Gap We Noticed

There's a specific category that's absent across your entire snack and pickle range: Kerala-origin ambient products. Banana chips, jackfruit chips, tapioca chips, Kerala mixtures, traditional Kerala pickles. Not a niche within South Indian food — the single biggest-selling South Indian snack format in the UK and EU market by volume.

Your snack range covers North Indian formats comprehensively. Tamil Nadu and Andhra products appear through some of the brands you carry. But the Kerala ambient snack category — the one format that travels without refrigeration, carries strong identity, and has no European own-brand competitor — has no label in your range at all.

That's the gap. It sits inside your existing South Indian customer base, not in a new one you'd need to build.

Why This Category Moves

Kerala snacks travel well. Banana chips, jackfruit chips, and tapioca products are all dry ambient formats with shelf lives that work for European distribution. They're not chilled, not frozen, not fragile. Logistics are clean.

The diaspora demand is already there. Your South Indian and Kerala customers currently drive to specialist online shops or across borders to source these products. They're not buying them from your shelves because there's nothing to buy. The category has a proven pull in every European market with a meaningful South Indian community — and the Netherlands has one of the stronger ones on the continent.

No distributor in the Netherlands currently carries a branded Kerala ambient snack range from origin. The brands in the MTR and Priya range don't cover this. Balaji and Karachi Bakery don't touch it. The shelf section doesn't exist yet in most distributors' catalogues. First-mover positioning in a category like this tends to hold.
