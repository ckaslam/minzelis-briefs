Current Range Analysis

Your range reflects a clear and deliberate strategy to anchor South Indian staples — Palakadan Matta Rice, Ponni, Jeeraga Samba, and an active pickles and papadam section all point to a buyer who understands what South Indian households actually cook from. The snack range reinforces this with a strong Tamil Nadu and Andhra bias, built around A2B and GRB: murukku in multiple formats, thattai, kara boondi, omapodi, and mixtures that cover Madras and Andhra profiles well.

That makes the gap that follows particularly visible, because the groundwork is already there.

The Commercial Gap
Your current South Indian snack range covers Tamil Nadu and Andhra formats well. The Kerala ambient snack category — banana chips in multiple flavour variants, jackfruit chips, tapioca chips, pakkavada, Kerala-style mixtures — is absent entirely, and so is a Kerala-branded pickle range to sit alongside Priya.
The Minzelis Solution

Minzelis is the category gap, not a replacement for what you already carry. A2B and GRB cover their segment well, and that does not change. What they do not cover is Kerala: the banana chip with eight distinct flavour variants, the jackfruit chip that has no equivalent on the UK South Asian shelf, the tapioca formats that Kerala diaspora buyers actively seek out. These are not incremental additions to your snack range. They are a separate subcategory with its own loyal customer base.

The same logic applies to pickles. Priya covers a broad South Indian and North Indian pickle range. Kerala-specific formats — Kaduku Mango, Tender Mango, Gooseberry — are a distinct customer request that sits alongside Priya rather than competing with it. Your pickles section can hold both without conflict.
