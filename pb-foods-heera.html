Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

P&B Foods has built one of the most complete South Asian ambient ranges in UK independent distribution. Heera covers over 100 spice SKUs, a full pulse and dal line, rice, flours, ghee, oils, pastes, and a growing drinks range. Rishta adds a premium pickle and chutney layer with over 30 flavours, covering mango, lime, carrot, garlic, and a range of traditional Gujarati and North Indian formats. The breadth across five brand lines — Heera, Rishta, Balah, Parivar, and RMD Red Circle — gives P&B reach from value foodservice right through to premium retail.

The Gap We Noticed

Across all five of your brand lines, there is no ambient snack category. No banana chips, no jackfruit chips, no tapioca chips, no Kerala mixture, no murukku, no pakkavada, no achappam. The South Indian ambient snack shelf is completely absent from the P&B Foods portfolio.

This is not a niche gap. Kerala and South Indian ambient snacks are a distinct, high-frequency purchase for the South Indian diaspora in the UK, and that diaspora is sizeable. Your accounts that serve South Indian and Keralite households — in Leicester, London, Birmingham, Manchester — are currently sourcing this category from elsewhere, outside the P&B relationship.

On the pickle side, Rishta is strong. But every SKU in the range is Gujarati or North Indian in format: mixed pickle, mango mild, lime, garlic, gunda, gorkeri. The Kerala pickle formats — kaduku mango, tender mango, raw mango Kerala-style — are not represented. For accounts with a South Indian customer base, that is a separate, unfilled section of the pickle fixture.

Why This Category Moves

The UK's South Indian and Keralite diaspora is concentrated in a handful of cities, and the community is deeply food-loyal. Banana chips, jackfruit chips, and Kerala-style mixtures are not impulse buys for this customer — they are a weekly staple purchase. The typical basket looks very different from a North Indian shopper's: less bread and spice, more rice, dal, coconut, and these specific ambient snacks. When a store doesn't carry them, that shopper goes somewhere that does.

The supply-side picture reinforces the opportunity. The major ambient Indian food brands that your accounts stock — Heera, TRS, East End, Kohinoor — do not make Kerala ambient snacks. There is no brand equivalent of Heera in this category. Accounts that want to serve their South Indian customers are patching with small, inconsistent suppliers. A named, branded, UK shelf-ready range from a verified 16-country export operation fills that gap cleanly.

P&B already exports to 24 countries. The export infrastructure, the relationships, and the route-to-market experience is already in place. Adding a South Indian snack range to the portfolio is a natural category extension, not a step into unknown territory.
