Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

Star Food Impex carries one of the most comprehensive South Asian food ranges in northern Germany. TRS, MDH, Haldiram, Shan, Tilda, Aashirvaadd, Wagh Bakri, Gits, Britannia, Parle, Tata, Dabur — across spices, pulses, flour, snacks, ready mixes, and pantry, the range is well-stocked and serves a diverse customer base across retail and foodservice from Hamburg to Frankfurt.

The import operation behind it confirms the picture: 1,560 recorded import shipments from India, and a harbour warehouse at Hamburg port built for exactly this kind of direct sourcing.

The Gap We Noticed

The brands you carry — strong as they are — are anchored in pan-Indian and North Indian categories. TRS leads in pulses and flours. MDH and Shan own the spice blend shelf. Haldiram covers the general Indian snack category. Gits covers South Indian in the ready-mix aisle.

What none of them cover is Kerala ambient. Banana chips, jackfruit chips, tapioca chips, the traditional South Indian mixture styles (Kerala Mixture, Malabar Mixture, Calicut Mixture), and the pickles and condiments that go with them — mango pickle, lime pickle, tender mango — these are a distinct sub-category that sits outside what the brands you currently carry produce.

The Confectionery & Snacks and Pickles and Seasonings categories are both live on your website. The Kerala layer within those categories is currently unrepresented in your range.

Why This Category Moves

The Malayali diaspora in Germany — Hamburg, Frankfurt, Munich, Cologne — is a distinct buyer group within the broader South Asian retail customer base. They shop in the same stores your retail customers supply, and they look for specific things: banana chips from Calicut, the traditional Kerala mixtures, tapioca preparations, and the pickles and condiments tied to Kerala cooking. These are not interchangeable with the North Indian snack category.

Your existing customers who supply South Asian retail stores already see this pull. The call for "Kerala snacks" or "banana chips" is a consistent request that the current ranging does not satisfy. Gits handles the ready-meal end of South Indian food. No brand in your current portfolio handles the ambient snack and pickle end.

The category also travels well in the wholesale channel — ambient, long shelf life, consistent repurchase. Customers who buy banana chips buy them weekly, not occasionally. For a distributor with 7 locations and a 350km delivery radius, that kind of repurchase behaviour is straightforward to build into recurring orders.
