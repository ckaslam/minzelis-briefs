Category Opportunity Brief, South Indian and Kerala Ambient Food

Current Range Analysis

Surya's South Asian range reflects a clear and deliberate strategy: anchor the mainstream with dominant rice brands, then build outward across cuisines. Laila and Salaam hold a commanding position in UK branded dry rice, while BIKAJI brings the North Indian snack segment credibly, covering the bhujia, sev, and North Indian mixture formats that resonate with a broad South Asian audience. Karma Bites signals a sharper push into the better-for-you snacking adjacency. The result is a well-constructed South Asian shelf that performs strongly at the mainstream and North Indian end of the category.

The Commercial Gap
BIKAJI covers the North Indian snack shelf. The South Indian side, banana chips, tapioca, jackfruit, regional mixtures, and murukku, has no equivalent branded line anywhere in the current range. That gap exists at scale in both the major multiples and across independent South Asian retail.
The Minzelis Solution

Minzelis is the South Indian equivalent of what BIKAJI does for the North Indian shelf. Where BIKAJI owns bhujia and sev, Minzelis owns banana chips, tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, Kerala mixture, and murukku variants. The two brands sit in the same section, serve the same buyer journey, and do not overlap on a single SKU.

The range goes beyond snacks. Alongside 51 snack SKUs, Minzelis carries 10 South Indian pickle lines and 12 pulse varieties, which means a single conversation covers the entire South Indian ambient shelf rather than one format within it.

Minzelis is managed from Germany and backed by Gramarhein's export-proven infrastructure, actively shipping to 16 countries, giving you an EU-compliant route to capture this category gap without the supplier risk that comes with a new-to-market brand.
