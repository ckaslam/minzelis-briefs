Category Opportunity Brief — South Indian & Kerala Ambient Food
Your Current Range

Transfood Grosshandel carries one of the more comprehensive Indian food ranges in NRW, with strong coverage across ambient grocery, spices, and pantry staples. Brands like MTR, Priya, TRS, Heera, Haldirams, MDH, Shan, and Pataks cover the core of what the South Asian retail customer expects from a well-stocked Indian wholesaler.

The South Indian sub-category is present — MTR and Priya both address it — and Transfood's own Annam label extends into some Kerala staples too. The range is broad and the buying operation is clearly strong, with 829 recorded India import shipments on file.

The Gap We Noticed

What the range doesn't yet have is a premium branded Kerala snack and pantry line — one where the brand itself is the reason the customer picks it off the shelf, not just the category.

MTR and Priya cover South Indian ready meals, chutneys, and cooking pastes well. But the ambient Kerala snack category — banana chips in multiple variants, tapioca chips, jackfruit chips, regional Kerala mixtures, murukku varieties — isn't represented under a standalone quality brand. These are the SKUs that Kerala and South Indian diaspora customers have specific preferences about, and where a recognised branded product consistently outsells generic or unbranded options.

A branded Kerala snack range doesn't compete with what MTR or Priya do — it fills the adjacent shelf position that neither of them occupies.

Why This Category Moves

The Kerala and broader South Indian diaspora in Germany is concentrated in NRW and Bavaria, with a high density of Tamil, Malayali, and Telugu households who regularly source ambient snacks and pantry items from Indian grocery stores. For these buyers, this is not impulse purchase territory — banana chips and Kerala mixtures are regular household items, bought frequently and brand-specifically.

The demand signal is already in your customer base. The brands they're asking for at retail level — familiar Kerala names, clean labelling, consistent quality — aren't consistently stocked in the wholesale channel in Germany. That's the gap between what the end consumer wants and what the supply chain currently carries.

None of the major brands in your current range address this directly. TRS doesn't make Kerala-style murukku. Shan has no Kerala snack category. Haldirams' snack range is North Indian in orientation. The branded Kerala snack shelf position is structurally open, and it tends to stay that way until a distributor actively fills it.
